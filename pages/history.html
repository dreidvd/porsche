Porsche History

Discover the rich heritage and legendary milestones that have shaped Porsche into the iconic brand it is today. From the first 356 to the revolutionary Taycan, explore our journey of innovation and excellence.

The Beginning

Francesco Porsche founded the company in 1931 as "Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche GmbH" in Stuttgart, Germany. Initially, the company provided consulting and development work for other automotive manufacturers.

1931

First Porsche Car

The legendary Porsche 356 was introduced, becoming the first production car to bear the Porsche name. This rear-engine sports car set the foundation for Porsche's design philosophy and established the brand's reputation for performance and reliability.

1948

The Iconic 911

The Porsche 911 made its debut at the Frankfurt Motor Show, revolutionizing the sports car world. With its distinctive silhouette and rear-engine layout, the 911 became an instant icon and remains in production today as one of the most successful sports cars ever made.

1963

Le Mans Dominance

Porsche achieved its first overall victory at the 24 Hours of Le Mans with the 917, beginning a legacy of endurance racing excellence. This victory marked the start of Porsche's dominance in motorsport, with 19 overall Le Mans victories to date.

1970

Innovation in Motorsport

The Porsche 956 and 962 became legends in Group C racing, winning multiple championships and setting new standards for endurance racing. These cars showcased Porsche's engineering excellence and commitment to pushing the boundaries of performance.

1982

Modern Era Begins

The introduction of the Boxster marked Porsche's entry into the mid-engine sports car segment, bringing the brand's performance and quality to a broader audience while maintaining the company's core values of driving pleasure and engineering excellence.

1996

SUV Revolution

The Cayenne SUV was introduced, expanding Porsche's lineup beyond sports cars. Despite initial skepticism, the Cayenne became a massive success, proving that Porsche could maintain its performance DNA while creating practical, everyday vehicles.

2002

Electric Future

The Taycan was launched as Porsche's first fully electric vehicle, combining cutting-edge electric technology with the brand's signature performance and driving dynamics. The Taycan represents Porsche's commitment to sustainable mobility without compromising on the driving experience.

2019
